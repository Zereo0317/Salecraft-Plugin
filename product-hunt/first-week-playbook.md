# SaleCraft -- First Week Launch Playbook

## Pre-Launch (D-7 to D-1)

### D-7: Foundation

**Teaser content:**
- Post on Twitter/X: "Next week I'm launching something for every small product seller who's tired of paying $3,000/month to a marketing agency. It starts with a free consultation -- no catch." Include a blurred screenshot of the SaleCraft chat interface.
- LinkedIn post: Share the origin story (condensed version of maker's comment). Focus on the problem, not the solution yet.

**Hunter selection strategy:**
- Target hunters with history in AI tools, marketing, or e-commerce categories.
- Priority list: hunters who launched Jasper, Copy.ai, Unbounce, Carrd, or similar marketing/landing page tools.
- DM approach: "Hey [name], I've been building an AI marketing consultant that works on any AI platform -- free consultation, paid only for execution. Would you be interested in hunting it? Happy to give you early access and a walkthrough."
- Backup: Self-hunt is fine on PH. If no hunter confirms by D-3, plan to self-hunt.

**Build the "upvote army" (ethical version):**
- Make a spreadsheet of 100+ contacts: indie hacker friends, Twitter mutuals, Slack/Discord community members, past beta users.
- DO NOT ask for upvotes. Ask them to "check it out on launch day and share honest feedback." People who genuinely try it will upvote naturally.

### D-5: Social Proof Setup

- Collect 3-5 beta user testimonials with specific numbers ("saved me 12 hours," "landing page converted 4.2%," "$67 vs my old agency's $1,200").
- Create a short Loom video (2 min) showing: paste repo URL into ChatGPT -> get brand audit -> see strategy -> generate LP. No narration fluff, just the flow.
- Prepare all PH gallery images (see launch-brief.md, 5 slides).

### D-3: Email Draft

**Subject:** I built something for you -- launching Tuesday on Product Hunt

**Body:**
> Hey [name],
>
> Quick note -- I'm launching SaleCraft on Product Hunt this [day].
>
> It's an AI marketing consultant for physical product sellers. The twist: the consultation is completely free. You paste a GitHub link into any AI you already use (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini), and it becomes your marketing consultant -- brand audit, competitor analysis, growth strategy. No account needed.
>
> If you want execution (landing pages, reels, ads), that's the paid part. But most of the value is free.
>
> I'd really appreciate it if you could check it out on launch day and leave honest feedback. Here's the link (goes live at midnight PT): [PH link]
>
> Either way, you can try the free consultation right now:
> Paste https://github.com/connactai/Salecraft-Plugin into any AI chat.
>
> Thanks,
> [Name]

Send this email on D-2 evening so it's in inboxes on D-1 morning.

### D-1: Final Prep

- Schedule all social posts (see social-launch-posts.md) but DO NOT publish yet.
- Test the free consultation flow one more time on ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini. Make sure it works.
- Prepare 10 templated (but personalized) responses for common PH comments (see Launch Day section).
- Set alarm for 12:01 AM PT.
- Pre-write the PH first comment (see launch-brief.md).

## Launch Day (D-Day)

**00:15 - 01:00**
- DM your top 20 contacts: "Hey, we just launched! Would love your honest take: [link]"
- Monitor for the first few comments and respond within 5 minutes.

**06:00 - 07:00 (Morning US East Coast wakes up)**
- LinkedIn post goes live (the career-narrative one from social-launch-posts.md).
- Second tweet in the thread.
- Instagram story #1.

**08:00 - 09:00**
- Share in Shopify-related communities and forums.
- Post on Indie Hackers.
- Check PH ranking -- respond to every single comment.

**10:00 - 12:00**
- If trending top 5: share a "thank you" update tweet with a screenshot of the ranking.
- If not trending: double down on personal outreach. DM 20 more contacts.
- Post the Reddit r/SideProject post (see reddit-posts.md).

**12:00 - 14:00 (US lunch break)**
- Share behind-the-scenes content on Twitter: "The hardest part of building SaleCraft was..." or "Fun fact: SaleCraft speaks 15 languages because..."

**15:00 - 17:00**
- Post the Reddit r/Entrepreneur post.

**18:00 - 20:00**
- Evening push: "Last few hours to support us on Product Hunt! Here's what users are saying: [quote]"

**21:00 - 23:59**
- Final engagement push. Thank everyone who commented.
- Prepare D+1 content.

### Comment Response Templates

**"How is this different from [competitor]?"**
> Great question! Most AI marketing tools jump straight to generating content. SaleCraft starts with a free consultation -- it diagnoses your brand, runs a competitor scan, and builds a strategy before touching any creative. Think of it as "marketing consultant first, tool second." Also, it works on any AI platform you already use -- no new app to install.

**"Is the free part actually free?"**
> 100% free, no catch. 25 of our 35 skills are completely free -- brand audit, strategy, funnel design, competitor analysis, growth planning. No account, no credit card, no trial period. You can use the free consultation forever. You only pay if you want the AI to actually build something (landing pages, reels, ads).

**"What languages do you support?"**
> 15+ languages: English, Traditional Chinese, Simplified Chinese, Japanese, Korean, Vietnamese, French, Thai, Spanish, Portuguese, Arabic (with proper RTL layout), German, Indonesian, Malay, and Hindi. It's not just translation -- the AI adapts colors, typography, and trust signals for each culture.

**"Can I see an example?"**
> Absolutely! [Share a link to a demo LP]. This was generated in about 30 minutes for a skincare brand. The AI ran a brand audit first, then generated a 10-page landing page with culturally adapted visuals.

**Technical/skeptical comment:**
> Fair point. [Address the specific concern directly]. I built this because [connect to personal motivation]. Happy to jump on a quick call if you want to dig deeper -- DM me!

**Generic praise ("Looks great!"):**
> Thanks! Have you tried the free consultation yet? Just paste the GitHub link into any AI and tell it what you sell -- I'd love to hear what it comes up with for your product.

### Engagement Tactics

- **Ask questions back.** Every response should end with a question to drive reply threads.
- **Share specific numbers.** "Our generated LPs average 3.2x conversion rate" is better than "it works well."
- **Upvote other launches.** Be a good PH citizen. Comment genuinely on 5-10 other launches throughout the day.
- **Screenshot and share.** If someone posts a great comment, screenshot it and share on Twitter with credit.

## Post-Launch (D+1 to D+7)

### D+1: Momentum

**Content:**
- Twitter thread: "What we learned from launching on Product Hunt (with real numbers)" -- share upvote count, comment count, traffic stats, conversion rate.
- Respond to any remaining PH comments.
- Post Reddit r/Shopify post (see reddit-posts.md).

**Converting PH visitors:**
- Anyone who signed up during launch day gets a personal welcome email within 24 hours.
- Set up a simple onboarding sequence: Day 1 email = "Try the free brand audit." Day 3 = "Here's what other sellers discovered." Day 7 = "Ready to build? Here's a walkthrough."

### D+2: Testimonial Collection

- DM every user who tried the product during launch: "Hey! Thanks for checking out SaleCraft. Would you mind sharing a quick sentence about your experience? Even 'it was interesting because...' helps a ton."
- Offer to run a free deep-dive brand audit for anyone willing to do a case study.
- Post the best testimonial on Twitter/LinkedIn with the user's permission.

### D+3: Content Repurposing

Turn launch day content into new formats:
- **Blog post:** "How We Built an AI Marketing Consultant That Works on Any AI Platform" (technical angle for Hacker News).
- **Twitter thread:** Extract 5 key insights from PH comments and respond to each.
- **Short video (Reels/TikTok):** 30-second screen recording of the free consultation in action. Caption: "I asked AI to audit my brand for free. Here's what it found."

### D+4: Community Seeding

- Answer questions on relevant Reddit threads (don't promote -- genuinely help, mention SaleCraft only if directly relevant).
- Join 2-3 Shopify/e-commerce Facebook groups. Provide value first.
- Post in Indie Hackers with a detailed "building in public" update.

### D+5: Partner Outreach

- Reach out to 5-10 e-commerce newsletters for potential features.
- Contact 3-5 YouTube creators who review AI tools.
- Offer exclusive deals for their audiences (e.g., "1,000 bonus points for [creator]'s audience").

### D+6: Data Analysis

- Analyze launch week metrics: PH upvotes, website traffic, signups, free consultation starts, paid conversions.
- Identify which channel drove the most qualified users (not just traffic).
- Write up findings for internal use and for a future "building in public" post.

### D+7: Retrospective + Next Sprint

- Publish a "1 week after Product Hunt" post on Twitter/LinkedIn with honest numbers.
- Address the top 3 pieces of feedback from launch week.
- Plan the next feature or improvement based on user feedback.
- Set up a recurring content calendar for the next month.

## Emergency Playbook

**If ranking drops below top 10 by noon:**
- Activate secondary contact list (people you held in reserve).
- Post a genuine "we need your support" message in closest communities.
- Share a surprising stat or user quote to re-energize attention.

**If there's a technical issue on launch day:**
- Acknowledge it immediately on PH: "We're seeing some load from the PH traffic (great problem to have!) -- fixing now."
- Free consultation always works (it's just a GitHub repo + conversation). Direct people there while fixing paid features.

**If negative feedback dominates:**
- Respond to every concern with empathy and specifics.
- If the criticism is valid, say so publicly: "You're right, and here's how we're fixing it."
- Never get defensive. PH audiences respect honesty over polish.
