# Content & Sensory Engineering for Product Marketing

Apply this skill when a physical product seller needs to craft marketing content that converts -- from psychological profiling of the target audience through narrative design, sensory mapping, and final copy optimization. This skill is FREE, no credits needed.

For detailed frameworks, the 5 awareness levels, narrative templates, and sensory mapping tables, load `references/toolkit.md`.

---

## Core Principle

> Content engineering is not "writing well." It is designing the minimum viable message that moves THIS specific target, in THIS specific context, to take THIS specific action. Every word earns its place or gets cut.

---

## The 4-Layer Message Architecture

Build every piece of marketing content through these four layers, in order:

### Layer 1: Psychological Profile (WHO)

Before writing a single word, complete the target profile from `prism-influence` skill:
- Decision style: analytical / intuitive / social-proof-driven
- Pain sensitivity: loss-averse vs. gain-seeking
- Awareness level (Schwartz): unaware → problem-aware → solution-aware → product-aware → most-aware

**Awareness determines everything:**

| Level | What They Need | Content Type | Opening Pattern |
|-------|---------------|-------------|----------------|
| Unaware | Education about the problem | Blog, social content | "你知道嗎？90% 的人..." |
| Problem-aware | Validation that a solution exists | Comparison, guide | "如果你也有這個困擾..." |
| Solution-aware | Why YOUR solution is best | Product page, LP | "跟其他方案不同的是..." |
| Product-aware | Proof and reassurance | Testimonials, FAQ | "2,000+ 顧客已經驗證..." |
| Most-aware | Deal and urgency | Offer page, ad | "限時優惠，立即行動" |

### Layer 2: Rhetorical Design (HOW to argue)

Select the Ethos/Pathos/Logos ratio based on the target profile:

| Target Profile | Ratio | Example |
|---------------|-------|---------|
| Analytical buyer (high-C) | 20E / 20P / 60L | Data-heavy, comparison tables, clinical results |
| Emotional buyer (high-N) | 30E / 50P / 20L | Story-driven, fear/hope arc, testimonials |
| Status buyer (high-E) | 40E / 40P / 20L | Authority signals, exclusivity, social proof |
| Community buyer (high-A) | 20E / 50P / 30L | Belonging narrative, user community, shared values |

### Layer 3: Narrative Structure (WHAT shape)

Choose a narrative skeleton based on content format:

**StoryBrand 7-Step (for LP/product pages):**
1. Character (customer) has a Problem
2. Meets a Guide (your brand) with Empathy + Authority
3. Guide gives a Plan (3-step)
4. Calls to Action (direct + transitional)
5. Shows Success (transformation)
6. Shows Failure (stakes of inaction)
7. Ends with Transformation (identity shift)

**Hero's Journey Micro-Arc (for social content/ads):**
1. Hook: the ordinary world (customer's current pain)
2. Call: the possibility of change
3. Transformation: the product as catalyst
4. Return: the new reality after using the product

**Slippery Slope (for sales copy):**
Every sentence's only job is to make the reader read the next sentence. No sentence should be removable without breaking the flow.

### Layer 4: Sensory Design (WHAT it feels like)

#### Color Psychology for Products

| Color Family | Emotion | Best For | Example |
|-------------|---------|----------|---------|
| Warm red/coral | Urgency, appetite | Food, flash sales, CTA buttons | 餐廳、限時搶購 |
| Deep green | Natural, trust, health | Organic, wellness, finance | 有機保養、健康食品 |
| Navy/dark blue | Authority, reliability | Tech, medical, professional | 醫美、專業設備 |
| Gold/champagne | Premium, luxury | High-end products | 精品、頂級保養 |
| Pastel/soft | Gentle, feminine | Baby, skincare, wellness | 嬰兒用品、敏感肌 |
| Black + white | Modern, minimal | Fashion, design, luxury | 設計品牌、時尚 |

#### Typography Emotion Loading

| Font Style | Conveys | Use When |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| Serif (Garamond, Times) | Tradition, authority, luxury | Heritage brands, premium products |
| Sans-serif (Helvetica, Inter) | Modern, clean, neutral | Tech, health, mass market |
| Handwritten/Script | Personal, artisan, warmth | Handmade, bakery, organic |
| Geometric/Technical | Innovation, precision | Tech gadgets, machinery |
| Display/Bold | Impact, confidence | Headlines, CTAs, sale events |

## Quick-Start: 5-Minute Content Audit

For any existing product copy, run this diagnostic:

1. **Awareness match**: Does the opening line match where the customer IS, not where you want them to be?
2. **First 8 words**: Would a stranger keep reading? (Slippery slope test)
3. **Benefit vs. feature ratio**: Count benefit statements vs. feature lists. Should be 3:1 minimum.
4. **Social proof placement**: Is proof near the point of highest doubt (usually near CTA)?
5. **CTA clarity**: Can a 12-year-old understand what happens when they click?
6. **Sensory coherence**: Do colors, fonts, and imagery tell the same emotional story as the words?

## Ethical Guardrails

- Never use dark patterns (false urgency, hidden costs, confusing opt-outs)
- Scarcity must be real; fabricated scarcity destroys trust permanently
- Emotional appeals must be truthful; never exaggerate product capabilities
- Testimonials must be genuine; fabricated reviews violate consumer protection laws
- Sensory design should enhance clarity, not obscure information
