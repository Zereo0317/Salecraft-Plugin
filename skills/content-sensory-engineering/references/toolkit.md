## 1. Schwartz's 5 Awareness Levels -- Complete Templates

Eugene Schwartz's *Breakthrough Advertising* (1966) defines five levels of customer awareness. Each level requires a fundamentally different opening strategy. Mismatching the opening to the awareness level is the #1 reason product copy fails.

### Level 1: Unaware

The customer does not know they have a problem. Your copy must create awareness without mentioning your product.

**Opening patterns**:

```
Pattern A: Shocking Fact
"你知道嗎？你的枕頭用了 2 年，30% 的重量是塵蟎的屍體和排泄物。"

Pattern B: Story Lead
"小玲一直以為自己只是過敏體質。直到她換了一個東西，症狀消失了。"

Pattern C: Question Lead
"為什麼你每天早上起來都鼻塞？不是感冒，不是過敏，而是⋯⋯"

Pattern D: Empathy Lead
"你可能覺得早上鼻塞是正常的。很多人都這麼以為。"
```

**Structure for Unaware audience**:
```
1. Hook: Surprising fact or emotional story (0% about your product)
2. Problem education: Explain the problem they didn't know they had
3. Consequences: What happens if they ignore this
4. Solution category: Introduce the TYPE of solution (not your brand yet)
5. Your product: Finally introduce as the best version of that solution
6. CTA: "Learn more" (not "Buy now" -- they just discovered the problem)
```

**Best channels**: Blog posts, educational social content, PR/media coverage, SEO content targeting informational queries. Never use paid ads for Unaware -- cost per conversion is too high.

### Level 2: Problem-Aware

The customer knows the problem but doesn't know solutions exist or which solution category to pursue.

**Opening patterns**:

```
Pattern A: Problem Agitation
"敏感肌反覆發作，你是不是已經試過抗敏乳液、換了洗面乳、
甚至去看了皮膚科——但每到換季又復發？"

Pattern B: "What if" Bridge
"如果有一種保養品，能讓你的敏感肌從『反覆發作』變成『穩定不復發』呢？"

Pattern C: Mistake Lead
"90% 的敏感肌患者都犯了同一個錯：用『治標不治本』的方式保養。"
```

**Structure for Problem-Aware audience**:
```
1. Hook: Validate their problem (show you understand their struggle)
2. Agitate: Show why their current approach isn't working
3. Reframe: Introduce a new way to think about the solution
4. Solution reveal: Present your solution category
5. Your product: Position as the optimal version
6. Evidence: Social proof + data
7. CTA: "Free trial / sample" or "Learn how it works"
```

### Level 3: Solution-Aware

The customer knows what type of solution they need but hasn't found or committed to a specific product.

**Opening patterns**:

```
Pattern A: Differentiation Lead
"精華液百百種。但只有冷壓萃取的，才能保留 95% 活性成分。
大多數品牌用的化學萃取？只剩 60%。"

Pattern B: Selection Guide
"3 個方法判斷一瓶精華液值不值得買："

Pattern C: Comparison Lead
"你正在比較精華液。在你決定之前，有一件事大多數品牌不會告訴你⋯⋯"
```

**Structure for Solution-Aware audience**:
```
1. Hook: Acknowledge they're comparing options
2. Differentiator: What makes your approach unique
3. Comparison: Your product vs alternatives (table format)
4. Proof: Certifications + testimonials + data
5. Objection handling: Address the top 3 concerns
6. CTA: "Compare now" or "Try risk-free"
```

### Level 4: Product-Aware

The customer knows your product but hasn't purchased. They need the final push.

**Opening patterns**:

```
Pattern A: Social Proof Hammer
"4,847 位回購客人。4.8/5.0 星。30 天不滿意全額退費。
你在猶豫什麼？"

Pattern B: Objection Pre-empt
"你可能在想：『真的有那麼好嗎？』讓這 126 則真實評價回答你。"

Pattern C: Time-Sensitive Offer
"你已經看了 3 次了（我們知道）。
這次用這個專屬折扣碼，省 $200。限時 48 小時。"
```

### Level 5: Most-Aware

The customer has purchased before or is 100% ready. They just need the deal.

**Opening patterns**:

```
Pattern A: Loyalty Recognition
"嗨！身為回購客人，這個月有你的專屬好康⋯⋯"

Pattern B: New Product for Existing Customer
"你愛用的玫瑰精華液，現在有升級版了。配方加量不加價。"

Pattern C: Pure Offer
"老客戶限定：85 折 + 免運。剩 48 小時。直接下單 →"
```

## 2. Gary Halbert's "Hungry Crowd" Theory

From *The Boron Letters*: "If you and I both owned a hamburger stand, and you could have any advantage -- better buns, better meat, better location -- what would I choose? A starving crowd."

The single most important advantage in marketing is a group of people who desperately want what you're selling. Not better copy, not a bigger budget -- demand.

### Halbert's 3 Questions for Product Sellers

Before writing any copy, answer:
1. **Who is starving for this?** Not "who might be interested" -- who NEEDS this NOW?
2. **Where are they gathered?** Which platforms, communities, moments?
3. **What are they already searching for?** Match their exact language.

### Hunger Signals by Product Category

| Category | Hunger Signal | Where to Find Them | Trigger Moment |
|---|---|---|---|
| Skincare | "我的皮膚最近爆痘..." | PTT Beauty, Dcard, IG stories | Season change, stress period |
| Health food | "最近健檢報告紅字..." | Health forums, LINE groups | Post-health-check |
| Baby products | "寶寶半夜一直哭..." | 媽媽社團, BabyHome | Sleep regression phases |
| Fashion | "找不到適合的正式場合穿搭" | Dcard Fashion, IG explore | Wedding season, job interviews |
| Food/F&B | "XX 附近有什麼好吃的" | Google Maps, food blogs | Lunch/dinner time, weekend |
| Supplements | "最近常感冒/疲勞" | Health forums, pharmacy inquiries | Season change, post-illness |
| Home goods | "搬新家需要..." | Decor groups, Pinterest | Moving, renovation |

### Demand Validation Toolkit

| Method | Tool | What to Check | Green Light |
|---|---|---|---|
| Search volume | Google Keyword Planner, Ubersuggest | Monthly searches for product keywords | > 1,000/month for main keyword |
| Competition | Google search results | Number of ads + organic competitors | Some competition = proven demand |
| Social listening | IG/TikTok hashtag search | People discussing this problem/solution? | Active conversations + UGC |
| Community | Facebook groups, PTT, Dcard | People asking about this product type? | Real questions from real people |
| Pre-order test | Simple LP with email capture | Would people pay or join waitlist? | > 5% conversion on email capture |

## 3. Sugarman's Slippery Slope -- Complete Framework

Joseph Sugarman (*The Adweek Copywriting Handbook*): "The sole purpose of the first sentence is to get you to read the second sentence."

### Implementation Rules

1. **First sentence**: Maximum 8 words. Must create curiosity or tension.
2. **First paragraph**: Maximum 3 sentences. Establish the emotional hook.
3. **Every paragraph**: Must end with an unresolved thread.
4. **Subheads**: Must be readable as a standalone story (scan test).
5. **CTA**: Must feel like the natural conclusion, not an interruption.

### Slippery Slope Techniques

| Technique | How It Works | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Open loop** | Start a story, don't finish it | "接下來她做了一件所有皮膚科醫師都反對的事⋯⋯" |
| **Curiosity gap** | Promise information, delay delivery | "敏感肌的真正元兇不是你想的那個⋯⋯" |
| **Pattern interrupt** | Break expected flow | "等等。先別往下滑。我有一個問題要問你。" |
| **Bucket brigade** | Transition phrases that pull forward | "但是⋯⋯" / "更重要的是⋯⋯" / "這就是重點：" |
| **Fascination bullets** | Benefit-loaded bullet points | "為什麼你的精華液總是『沒感覺』——答案在 pH 值" |
| **Cliff-hanger** | End a section mid-insight | "第三個步驟是最關鍵的——但大多數人都做錯了。" |

### Slippery Slope Structure for Product Copy

```
Line 1: Curiosity hook (unexpected fact or question)
  "你知道你的枕頭裡住著 200 萬隻塵蟎嗎？"

Line 2: Agitation (make the problem vivid)
  "牠們正在以你的皮屑為食，24 小時不停繁殖。"

Line 3: Stakes (what happens if you don't act)
  "這就是為什麼你的鼻子每天早上都塞住。"

Line 4: Turn (introduce the possibility of solution)
  "但如果有一個方法，能在你睡覺時消滅 99.7% 的塵蟎⋯⋯"

Line 5: Reveal (your product)
  "這正是 [Brand] 防蟎枕套做到的事。"
```

### Slippery Slope Test

Read your copy aloud. At each sentence, ask: "Would I keep reading?" If the answer is no at any point, rewrite that sentence. The copy fails at its weakest link.

| Luxury products | 50% | 35% | 15% | Brand authority + aspiration |
| Handmade/Artisan | 35% | 45% | 20% | Maker story + emotional connection |

### Building Ethos (Credibility) for Product Sellers

**Ethos hierarchy** (strongest to weakest):

1. **Third-party certification**: SGS, ECOCERT, FDA, ISO -- verifiable, cannot be faked
2. **Expert endorsement**: Named professional with checkable credentials
3. **Institutional affiliation**: University collaboration, hospital partnership
4. **Media coverage**: Publication mentions (earned, not paid)
5. **Industry awards**: Recognized competitions
6. **Years of operation**: "創立於 2015 年" -- time as trust signal
7. **Founder credentials**: Education, experience, background
8. **Production transparency**: Factory tours, ingredient sourcing documentation

### Building Pathos (Emotion) for Product Sellers

**Emotion map by product category**:

| Product | Primary Emotion | Secondary Emotion | Trigger Content |
|---|---|---|---|

| Food | Comfort + nostalgia | Guilt about health | Family meals, "媽媽的味道" |
| Fitness | Aspiration + pride | Shame about current state | Transformation stories |
| Fashion | Identity + belonging | Fear of being overlooked | Lifestyle shots |
| Home goods | Peace + control | Frustration with clutter | Organized space photos |

### Building Logos (Logic) for Product Sellers

**Evidence presentation hierarchy**:

| Evidence Type | Strength | Example |
|---|---|---|

| Brand claim | ★ | "我們的品質最好" (never use alone) |

## 5. Lakoff Framing -- Complete Application Guide

George Lakoff's core insight: **Never use the opponent's frame.** Whoever controls the frame controls the debate. When you argue within someone else's frame, you reinforce it even if you're disagreeing.

### Reframing for Product Marketing

| Competitor Attack | ❌ Their Frame (DON'T) | ✅ Your Frame (DO) |
|---|---|---|
| "Chemical-free is impossible" | "Our chemicals are safe" | "100% 天然植萃，成分你看得懂" |
| "Organic is just expensive placebo" | "Our organic products work" | "SGS 實證：28 天改善率 94%" |
| "Cheap alternatives are just as good" | "We're not that expensive" | "每天 $43 的投資，你的臉值得" |
| "Small brands can't guarantee quality" | "We have good quality control" | "SGS + ECOCERT 雙認證，比大品牌更透明" |
| "Natural products are weaker" | "Natural can be strong too" | "冷壓萃取保留 95% 活性——比化學萃取高 35%" |

### Frame Construction Rules

1. **Define your terms first** -- don't let competitors define the vocabulary
2. **Lead with your strengths** -- make them argue in YOUR frame
3. **Use sensory language** -- abstract frames are weak; vivid frames are strong
4. **Repeat your frame consistently** -- frame strength comes from repetition
5. **Never negate the opponent's frame** -- "We're NOT expensive" reinforces "expensive"

## 6. Campbell's Hero's Journey -- Extended Product Templates

### The 12 Stages for Brand Storytelling

| Stage | Original | Product Marketing Version | Content Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Ordinary World | Hero's normal life | Customer's life with the problem | Brand story |
| 2. Call to Adventure | Challenge appears | Customer realizes they need change | Problem content |
| 3. Refusal of the Call | Hero hesitates | "I've tried before, nothing works" | Objection handling |
| 4. Meeting the Mentor | Wise guide appears | Discovering your brand/expert | Authority content |
| 5. Crossing the Threshold | Hero commits | First purchase or trial | CTA, trial offer |
| 6. Tests, Allies | Hero faces challenges | Using the product, seeing initial results | Onboarding |
| 7. Approach to Inmost Cave | Deeper commitment | Integrating into routine | Retention content |
| 8. The Ordeal | Greatest challenge | Moment of doubt ("is this working?") | Day-14 reassurance |
| 9. Reward | Hero succeeds | Visible results, compliments | Testimonial solicitation |
| 10. The Road Back | Return to normal | Product becomes identity | Subscription |
| 11. Resurrection | Final transformation | Customer as advocate | UGC program |
| 12. Return with Elixir | Hero shares wisdom | Customer recommends to others | Referral program |

### Hero's Journey Email Sequence (7-Email Post-Purchase)

| Email | Stage | Subject Line | Content Focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 (Order confirm) | Crossing Threshold | "你的改變從今天開始" | Welcome + expectations |
| 2 (Day 3) | Tests begin | "第 3 天：你可能會注意到⋯⋯" | Setting expectations |
| 3 (Day 7) | First challenge | "很多人第 7 天會想放棄" | Reassurance + tips |
| 4 (Day 14) | The Ordeal | "堅持 14 天的你，真的很了不起" | Celebrate + others' results |
| 5 (Day 21) | Reward | "準備好聽到『你皮膚好好』了嗎？" | Social validation |
| 6 (Day 28) | Return | "28 天到了。感覺怎麼樣？" | Review request + repurchase |
| 7 (Day 35) | Elixir | "你身邊有人也需要這個改變嗎？" | Referral program |

### 3-Act Micro-Version (for Ads/Social/Reels)

```
Act 1 -- The Ordinary World (3 seconds)
Show the customer's current reality: the pain, the frustration, the "before."
→ "每天早上照鏡子，看到的是疲憊的自己..."

Act 2 -- The Journey (10 seconds)
The guide (brand) appears with a plan. Show the transformation in action.
→ "30 天，每天 30 秒，一滴精華液..."

Act 3 -- The Return (3 seconds)
The new reality. The customer is transformed. CTA is the invitation to begin.
→ "找回你的光澤。立即體驗 →"
```

## 7. StoryBrand 7-Step -- Industry Templates

### Template: Skincare Brand

```
Character: 「每天面對鏡子的你」
Problem:
  - External: 膚質暗沉、敏感反覆
  - Internal: 沒自信、覺得自己不值得被好好對待
  - Philosophical: 保養不該這麼複雜
Guide: 「我們是 [Brand]，由皮膚科醫師創立」
  - Empathy: 「我們懂你試過很多東西都沒用的挫折」
  - Authority: 「SGS 認證 + 醫師團隊研發」
Plan:
  1. 「做 30 秒膚質自測（免費）」
  2. 「收到為你挑選的試用組」
  3. 「28 天看到改變」
Call to Action:
  - Direct: 「立即免費自測 →」
  - Transitional: 「下載保養指南（免費）」
Failure: 「繼續用不適合的產品 → 膚況惡化 → 花更多冤枉錢」
Success: 「28 天後，收到人生第一句『你最近皮膚好好』」
```

```
Character: 「想給家人最好的你」
Problem:
  - External: 食品安全疑慮、不知道吃進什麼
  - Internal: 擔心對不起家人的健康
  - Philosophical: 吃東西不該需要冒險

  - Empathy: 「我們也是爸媽，懂你的擔心」

Failure: 「繼續猜自己吃的是什麼 → 食安新聞每週上演」

Failure: 「繼續將就大量生產的替代品」

## 9. Dark Patterns -- Full Brignull Taxonomy

Harry Brignull identified 12 deceptive design patterns. Physical product sellers must know these to AVOID them.

### 12 Patterns with Legal Boundaries

| # | Pattern | Description | Taiwan Law Risk | EU/US Risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Bait & Switch | Advertise one thing, deliver another | 公平交易法 §21 | FTC Act §5 |
| 2 | Confirmshaming | Guilt-tripping opt-out ("No thanks, I hate savings") | Reputation risk | Growing scrutiny |
| 3 | Disguised Ads | Ads that look like content | 公平交易法 §21 | FTC disclosure |
| 4 | Forced Continuity | Charging after trial without clear notice | 消保法 §19 | EU auto-renewal |
| 5 | Friend Spam | Accessing contacts for invites | 個資法 §20 | GDPR, CCPA |
| 6 | Hidden Costs | Revealing fees only at checkout | 消保法 §11 | EU Consumer Rights |
| 7 | Misdirection | Drawing attention from unfavorable options | Reputation risk | FTC scrutiny |
| 8 | Price Comparison Prevention | Making comparison hard | Reputation risk | Increasing regulation |
| 9 | Privacy Zuckering | Confusing privacy settings | 個資法 §7 | GDPR Art 25 |
| 10 | Roach Motel | Easy in, hard out | 消保法 §19 | EU/CA cancel laws |
| 11 | Sneak into Basket | Adding items without consent | 消保法 §11 | EU Consumer Rights |
| 12 | Trick Questions | Confusing double negatives | Reputation risk | Increasing regulation |

### Ethical Alternatives

| Dark Pattern | Ethical Alternative | Business Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Fake urgency | Real limited runs, seasonal | Lower conversion/impression but zero trust damage |
| Confirmshaming | Neutral: "不用了，謝謝" | Slight lower opt-in, brand integrity preserved |
| Hidden costs | Full price from first page | Higher initial bounce, 40% fewer abandoned carts |
| Forced continuity | 7-day renewal notice + 1-click cancel | 5-10% higher churn, 90% fewer complaints |
| Fake reviews | Real reviews + verified purchase badge | Slower accumulation, bulletproof credibility |

## 10. Copy Formula Index

### Complete Formula Reference

| Formula | Structure | Best For | Length |
|---|---|---|---|
| **AIDA** | Attention → Interest → Desire → Action | LP, long-form ads | Long |
| **PAS** | Problem → Agitate → Solve | Short ads, social | Short |
| **BAB** | Before → After → Bridge | Testimonials | Medium |
| **FAB** | Feature → Advantage → Benefit | Product descriptions | Short |
| **4U** | Useful, Urgent, Unique, Ultra-specific | Headlines | One line |
| **QUEST** | Qualify → Understand → Educate → Stimulate → Transition | Sales letters | Long |
| **Star-Chain-Hook** | Star (attention) → Chain (reasons) → Hook (CTA) | Email sequences | Medium |
| **PPPP** | Picture → Promise → Prove → Push | Visual-heavy ads | Medium |

### Headline Formula Templates

```
Number + Adjective + Keyword + Promise:
"5 個簡單步驟，28 天找回你的自信肌"

How to + Desired Outcome:
"如何用一瓶精華液取代你梳妝台上的 7 瓶保養品"

"為什麼 87% 的皮膚科醫師自己都不用專櫃保養品？"
