# Social Theory & Power Analysis for Brand Positioning

Apply social theory frameworks to decode hidden power structures in markets and transform them into actionable brand positioning strategies for physical product sellers.

**This skill is 100% FREE. No credits are deducted.**

## Core Philosophy

> Markets are not neutral arenas. Every market has invisible power structures -- cultural hierarchies, information asymmetries, legitimacy gatekeepers, and attention monopolies. Understanding these structures is the difference between a brand that fights for scraps and one that rewrites the rules.

- When the user asks: "Why can't I compete with big brands?" / "How do I build brand authority?"
- When positioning a premium or culturally-charged product (beauty, health, food, fashion)

## The 6 Power Frameworks

Load the full theoretical foundation from `references/frameworks.md`. Below is the diagnostic application sequence.

### Framework Selection Logic

| User Situation | Primary Framework | Application |
|----------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Competing against industry giants | Marx: Commodity Fetishism | Expose what the giant hides; re-humanize the product |
| Building trust from zero | Weber: Authority Types | Choose an authority pathway (tradition / charisma / expertise) |
| Entering a culturally coded market (luxury, organic, artisan) | Bourdieu: Cultural Capital | Map the taste hierarchy; position within or against it |
| Platform-dependent distribution (IG, TikTok, Amazon) | Foucault: Panopticon + Castells: Network | Understand algorithmic surveillance; become a network switcher |
| Fighting misinformation or category skepticism | Habermas: Public Sphere + Vosoughi | Build rational trust in a post-truth environment |
| Navigating data-driven marketplaces | Zuboff: Surveillance Capitalism | Leverage or resist data extraction; privacy as differentiator |

## Phase 1: Power Audit (Diagnostic)

Ask the following five questions to map the user's structural position:

1. Which brands "set the rules" in the user's industry? (pricing, trends, standards)
2. How do consumers judge the product's quality? (certifications? KOL? reviews? price?)
3. Who controls the sales channels? (platform fees? algorithms? distributors?)
4. What can the user do that big brands cannot or will not?
5. Does the product carry a story that competitors cannot tell?

---

## Phase 2: Structural Analysis

After collecting answers, apply the 6 frameworks systematically. Reference `references/frameworks.md` for each framework's detailed application method.

Produce an analysis covering these six dimensions:

1. **Ideology Layer (Marx)** -- Industry narrative: who defines "good product"? What does commodity fetishism hide? Where is the opening?
2. **Authority Structure (Weber)** -- Traditional authority (heritage brands), charismatic authority (KOL, founder story), legal-rational authority (SGS, FDA, patents)
3. **Cultural Capital (Bourdieu)** -- Taste hierarchy in the category. Target audience habitus analysis.
4. **Platform Governance (Foucault + Castells)** -- Which platforms surveil the user? Network node analysis.
5. **Public Sphere (Habermas)** -- Rational discourse space in the industry or pure noise? Trust-building path.
6. **Data Governance (Zuboff)** -- Data asymmetry: what do platforms know that the user does not?

---

## Phase 3: Strategic Positioning Recommendation

Based on the structural analysis, recommend one of 5 positioning strategies:

| Strategy | When to Use | Example |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| **Counter-Narrative** | Industry narrative hides truth | Handmade soap vs industrial soap |
| **Cultural Insurgent** | Taste hierarchy excludes the user | Streetwear challenging luxury fashion |
| **Authority Builder** | No clear trust signal yet | SGS certification + educational content |
| **Network Switcher** | Trapped in one platform | Cross-platform + owned channels |
| **Transparency Play** | Industry trust is low | Open manufacturing, zero tracking |

Deliver: Primary strategy + Rationale + Concrete 3-step actions + Risk + Backup strategy.
