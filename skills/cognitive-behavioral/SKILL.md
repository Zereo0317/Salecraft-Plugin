## Dual-Process Theory Quick Reference

Every customer operates with two systems simultaneously. Your marketing must address both.

| Aspect | System 1 (Fast) | System 2 (Slow) |
|---|---|---|
| Speed | Milliseconds | Seconds to minutes |
| Effort | Effortless, automatic | Effortful, deliberate |
| Triggers | Visuals, emotions, patterns | Data, comparisons, calculations |
| Where it dominates | Social media scroll, first impression | Product page deep-read, price comparison |
| Marketing element | Hero image, headline, color, layout | Specs table, ingredient list, reviews |
| Product examples | 零食、飲料、平價飾品 | 保養品、保健食品、高價電子產品 |

**Design principle**: Capture System 1 first (hook), then satisfy System 2 (evidence). Never lead with data for impulse-buy products, and never skip evidence for high-involvement purchases.

## 12 Key Biases for Product Marketing Design

Quick-reference matrix -- use this to audit any LP, ad, or copy:

| # | Bias | Customer Behavior | Marketing Application | Caution |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Anchoring** | First number seen becomes reference point | Show original price before discount; show competitor's higher price first | Never fabricate the anchor price |
| 2 | **Availability** | Overweight vivid, recent, emotional info | Use vivid before/after photos; lead with dramatic testimonials | Don't rely solely on fear -- balance with positive outcomes |
| 3 | **Representativeness** | Judge by similarity to prototype | Make your packaging match the "premium" prototype of your category | Don't mislead about product quality through packaging alone |
| 4 | **Framing** | Same facts, different presentation = different decisions | "95% 活性成分保留" vs "僅 5% 成分流失" -- pick the frame that resonates | Both must be true; choose frame, don't fabricate facts |
| 5 | **Loss Aversion** | Losing $100 hurts 2x more than gaining $100 | "Don't miss out" > "Get a bonus"; "每天流失膠原蛋白" activates action | Don't create false fear -- use real losses |
| 6 | **Endowment Effect** | People value what they already "own" more | Free trials, samples, customization -- once they use it, they want to keep it | Ensure the trial experience matches the actual product |
| 7 | **Confirmation Bias** | Seek info that confirms existing beliefs | If customer believes "natural = better," lead with naturalness, then add science | Never exploit health misinformation |
| 8 | **Status Quo Bias** | Prefer current situation over change | Subscription default ON; make switching FROM competitor easy | Always allow easy cancellation |
| 9 | **Hyperbolic Discounting** | Prefer immediate rewards over future ones | "今天下單，明天到貨" beats "長期使用效果好" for first purchase | Pair with long-term value for retention |
| 10 | **Sunk Cost** | Continue because already invested | "你已經看了 7 頁，只差最後一步" -- leverage invested attention | Don't trap customers; always offer clean exit |
| 11 | **Base Rate Neglect** | Ignore base rates, focus on vivid cases | One dramatic testimonial > "statistically 89% satisfied" for System 1 | Always provide both story + stats for credibility |
| 12 | **Hindsight Bias** | "I knew it all along" after the fact | Post-purchase: "你的好眼光！" reinforces they made the right choice | Use for retention/review solicitation |

## Cialdini's 7 Principles -- Product Marketing Quick Card

| Principle | Trigger Phrase | Best For | Counter-Measure (smart customers notice) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Reciprocity** | "Here's something free for you" | Lead generation, first purchase | Gift feels manipulative if too calculated → make value genuine |
| **Commitment** | "You already started" | Funnel progression, upsell | Feels trapped → always show easy exit |
| **Social Proof** | "12,847 customers chose this" | Trust building, conversion | Fake reviews → use verifiable specifics |
| **Authority** | "Doctor-recommended" | High-involvement products | Credentials questioned → use third-party certs |
| **Liking** | "We understand you" | Brand loyalty, repeat purchase | Feels fake → be genuinely authentic |
| **Scarcity** | "Only 23 left" | Urgency, time-sensitive offers | Fake scarcity → customers check and leave forever |
| **Unity** | "We're part of the same community" | Tribe building, advocacy | Forced tribalism → build genuine shared identity |

## Nudge & Choice Architecture for Product Sellers

### NUDGES Framework Applied

| Letter | Principle | Product Marketing Application | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **N** | iNcentives | Align incentives with desired behavior | "回購省 15%、推薦朋友你也賺 $100" |
| **U** | Understand mapping | Help customers map choices to outcomes | "乾肌 → A 款；油肌 → B 款" decision helper |
| **D** | Defaults | Set the default to the best option | Subscription pre-selected; medium size as default |
| **G** | Give feedback | Show progress toward goal | "你的保養計畫完成 60%" / "再買 $200 免運" |
| **E** | Expect error | Design for mistakes | Easy return, easy size exchange, "你可能也需要" |
| **S** | Structure complex choices | Simplify overwhelming options | "最多人選" badge on one option; max 3-4 choices visible |

### Default Design (High-Impact Nudge)

Defaults drive 70-90% of choices. Design them ethically:

| Context | Default Setting | User Benefit | Business Benefit |
|---|---|---|---|
| Product page | Medium size pre-selected | Most popular = most likely to satisfy | Higher AOV than smallest |
| Bundle page | Recommended bundle highlighted | Curated decision saves time | Higher AOV |
| Checkout | Standard shipping (not express) | Lower cost, fewer returns | Better margin |
| Subscription | Monthly delivery | Consistency, no stockout anxiety | Predictable revenue |
| Email opt-in | Unchecked (ethical default) | Respects privacy | Higher quality list |

## Personality Profiling for Target Audience Design

### Big Five (OCEAN) -- Observation Cues for Product Sellers

You cannot give customers a personality test. Instead, infer from observable behavior:

| Trait | High-Trait Cues | Low-Trait Cues | Copy Adjustment |
|---|---|---|---|
| **O** (Openness) | Browses new arrivals first; follows indie brands; mentions "有趣/特別" | Always buys same product; asks "跟之前一樣嗎？" | High O: emphasize novelty; Low O: emphasize consistency |
| **C** (Conscientiousness) | Reads full specs; asks about ingredients; compares before buying | Impulse adds to cart; asks "推薦哪個就好" | High C: lead with data; Low C: lead with recommendation |
| **E** (Extraversion) | Shares purchases; tags brand; writes public reviews | Silent buyer; uses DM only; avoids community | High E: community features; Low E: personal benefit focus |
| **A** (Agreeableness) | Uses "我們/家人/朋友"; seeks harmony; avoids confrontation | Uses "我/最好的/贏"; competitive framing | High A: warmth + community; Low A: superiority positioning |
| **N** (Neuroticism) | Asks about returns first; reads all reviews; worried about side effects | Confident buyer; minimal risk checking | High N: safety + guarantee; Low N: opportunity + gains |

### Dark Triad Awareness (Recognize, Do Not Exploit)

Some customer segments exhibit Dark Triad traits. Recognize them to adapt communication, not to manipulate:

| Trait | Behavioral Cue | Ethical Marketing Response |
|---|---|---|
| **Narcissism** | "I deserve the best"; status-conscious; wants exclusive | Premium positioning + exclusivity are legitimate; avoid flattery traps |
| **Machiavellianism** | Strategic buyer; compares everything; seeks maximum value | Transparent pricing + value comparison; don't try to trick them -- they'll see through it |
| **Psychopathy** | Impulsive; low empathy; novelty-seeking; may abuse return policy | Clear terms, fair policies, strong boundaries; don't personalize emotional appeals |

## Social Proof & Conformity Design

### Conformity Types (from Asch, Milgram, Bandura)

| Type | Mechanism | Marketing Application |
|---|---|---|
| **Informational** (Asch) | "Others know something I don't" | "最多皮膚科醫師推薦" -- experts chose this, so it must be right |
| **Normative** (Asch) | "I want to fit in" | "加入 12,000 位會員" -- everyone's doing it |
| **Obedience** (Milgram) | "Authority says so" | "衛福部核准" / "SGS 認證" -- certified authority |
| **Vicarious** (Bandura) | "I saw someone like me succeed" | Before/after testimonials from similar customers |

### Social Proof Hierarchy for Product Pages

Deploy social proof in this order of decreasing strength:

1. **Expert endorsement** with credentials -- "皮膚科王醫師（台大醫院）推薦"
2. **Specific customer count** -- "累積 12,847 位回購客人"
3. **Named testimonials with photo + details** -- "小美 (28歲, 混合肌, 台北): 用了 3 週..."
4. **Aggregate rating** -- "4.8/5.0 (126 則評價)"
5. **Media mention** -- "女人我最大/GQ 推薦"
6. **Generic claims** -- "很多人愛用" (weakest -- avoid)

## Behavioral Design Pattern: The Product Page

Apply cognitive-behavioral principles to each section of a product LP:

```
Section 1: Hero
├── Bias used: Availability (vivid image), Framing (benefit headline)
├── System: System 1 capture
└── Pattern: "[Emotional outcome] + [beautiful product photo]"

Section 2: Problem
├── Bias used: Loss Aversion, Availability
├── System: System 1 → System 2 transition
└── Pattern: "Are you losing X? Here's what happens without action."

Section 3: Solution
├── Bias used: Anchoring (position as category leader), Framing
├── System: System 2 engagement
└── Pattern: Feature → Benefit → Evidence chain

Section 4: Social Proof
├── Bias used: Social Proof, Base Rate Neglect (vivid stories)
├── System: System 1 trust shortcut
└── Pattern: Number + Named Story + Expert Badge

Section 5: Comparison
├── Bias used: Anchoring (competitor as anchor), Framing
├── System: System 2 rationalization
└── Pattern: Table showing your product wins on key dimensions

Section 6: FAQ / Objection Handling
├── Bias used: Inoculation, Confirmation Bias (confirm their wise choice)
├── System: System 2 objection resolution
└── Pattern: "You might wonder... Here's the answer + evidence"

Section 7: CTA
├── Bias used: Loss Aversion (scarcity), Endowment (trial), Sunk Cost (you've read this far)
├── System: System 1 action trigger
└── Pattern: "[Clear action] + [Risk reversal] + [Urgency if genuine]"
```
