## Why This Matters for Product Sellers

The founder IS the brand's most powerful asset — especially for small to mid-size physical product brands. Consumers buy from people they trust. Retailers stock products from founders they believe in. Investors fund operators who command a room.

**Three truths:**
1. Your product's ceiling is your personal credibility ceiling
2. Every sales meeting, investor pitch, and media interview is a performance you can engineer
3. Your network determines your distribution — weak ties open doors that cold outreach cannot

## The 3-Axis Charisma Model (Cabane)

Every human interaction evaluates you on three axes simultaneously. All are trainable.

```
PRESENCE — Are you actually HERE? (attention, eye contact, listening)
    +
POWER — Can you ACT on your intentions? (status, competence, resources)
    +
WARMTH — Are your intentions GOOD? (empathy, generosity, care)
    =
CHARISMA (the compound effect)
```

### Self-Diagnostic

Ask 5 trusted contacts to rate you 1-10 on each axis. Your lowest axis is your highest-leverage improvement area.

| Axis | Low Score Signals | Improvement Actions |
|------|------------------|-------------------|
| Presence | Checking phone during conversation, interrupting, generic responses | 10-second eye contact practice, active listening drills, ban devices in meetings |
| Power | Hesitant speech, excessive hedging, poor posture | Speak in declarative sentences, controlled gestures, upright posture, credential display |
| Warmth | Transactional interactions, no personal interest shown, cold efficiency | Ask about THEM first, remember personal details, show genuine curiosity, strategic vulnerability |

### Context Calibration

| Situation | Lead With | Because |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| Selling to retailers | Power first, then Warmth | They need to trust your competence before caring about your story |
| Media interview | Warmth first, then Power | Audiences connect with humans, then respect expertise |
| Investor pitch | Power + Presence (equal), Warmth secondary | They evaluate capability and conviction |
| Customer interaction | Warmth first, Power through product | They want to feel cared for, then reassured |
| Negotiation with supplier | Presence + Power | They need to know you're serious and informed |

## Negotiation Toolkit (Voss + Fisher)

### Chris Voss: FBI Tactical Negotiation

| Technique | How | Product Seller Example |
|-----------|-----|----------------------|
| **Mirroring** | Repeat last 3 words as a question | Retailer: "Your margin is too high." You: "Too high?" (they elaborate) |
| **Labeling** | "It seems like..." / "It sounds like..." | "It sounds like shelf space is tight and you need proven sellers." |
| **Calibrated Questions** | "How am I supposed to...?" / "What would it take...?" | "What would it take for you to give us an end-cap display for 30 days?" |
| **Accusation Audit** | Preemptively list negatives | "You're probably thinking we're too small, too new, and too expensive. Let me address each." |
| **"That's right"** | Get them to say "That's right" (not "You're right") | Summarize their position until they say "That's right" — then they're aligned |
| **Anchor extreme** | Open with ambitious but defensible position | Set high initial wholesale price, then "concede" to your real target |

### Fisher/Ury: Principled Negotiation (Harvard)

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|------------|
| Separate people from the problem | Don't take price pushback personally; focus on the deal structure |
| Focus on interests, not positions | They say "lower price"; their interest might be "lower risk" → offer consignment |
| Generate options for mutual gain | "What if we do a trial run with a smaller order at this price?" |
| Use objective criteria | Industry standard margins, competitor pricing, volume benchmarks |
| Know your BATNA | Your best alternative to this deal. Never negotiate without one. |

## Mental Models for Brand Operators

### Top 10 Decision Models (from Munger/Farnam Street)

| Model | Application for Product Seller |
|-------|-------------------------------|
| **Inversion** | "What would definitely make my brand fail?" Then avoid those things. |
| **First Principles** | "What does my customer actually need?" Strip away industry assumptions. |
| **Second-Order Thinking** | "If I cut price, what happens AFTER the sale?" (lower perceived quality, margin erosion) |
| **Circle of Competence** | Stay in your domain. Don't diversify into things you don't understand. |
| **Opportunity Cost** | Every hour on Task A is an hour NOT on Task B. Ruthlessly prioritize. |
| **Leverage** | Which small action creates the biggest downstream effect? Do that first. |
| **Pareto (80/20)** | 20% of your SKUs likely drive 80% of revenue. Focus there. |
| **Map ≠ Territory** | Your business plan is not your business. Adapt when reality diverges. |
| **Hanlon's Razor** | Retailer didn't reply? Probably busy, not hostile. Don't escalate prematurely. |
| **Survivorship Bias** | Don't copy what successful brands did — understand why 99 others doing the same thing failed. |

## Network Strategy

### Granovetter's Weak Ties

Strong ties (close friends) give emotional support. **Weak ties** (acquaintances, industry contacts, conference connections) give **opportunities** — jobs, partnerships, distribution channels, press introductions.

### Ferrazzi's 5-per-Week Protocol

Contact 5 weak ties per week. Not to sell. Just to exist in their awareness.

| Week | Action | Time |
|------|--------|------|
| Mon | Comment thoughtfully on 2 industry contacts' LinkedIn posts | 10 min |
| Tue | Send 1 "thinking of you" message to a dormant contact | 5 min |
| Wed | Share 1 useful article with a contact who'd benefit | 5 min |
| Thu | Introduce 2 people in your network who should know each other | 10 min |
| Fri | Attend 1 virtual/physical industry event or community call | 30-60 min |

250 touchpoints/year. Compounding network effect.

## Founder Persona Construction

For physical product brands, the founder persona amplifies every marketing channel.

### The Founder Story Template

1. **Origin**: What personal experience led to creating this product?
2. **Struggle**: What obstacle did you overcome? (authenticity signal)
3. **Discovery**: What insight or breakthrough changed everything?
4. **Mission**: What change do you want to create in the world?
5. **Proof**: What evidence shows this mission is working?

### Persona-Brand Alignment Check

| If the brand is... | The founder should project... |
|--------------------|-----------------------------|
| Premium/luxury | Authority + taste + selectiveness |
| Organic/natural | Authenticity + passion + transparency |
| Innovative/tech | Expertise + vision + precision |
| Community-driven | Warmth + accessibility + shared identity |
| Heritage/traditional | Wisdom + continuity + craft mastery |
